For most motorised vehicles, there are three comonly used arrangements of parking spaces Parallel parking,perpendicular parking and angle parking.These are self-park configurations where the vehicle driver is able to access the parking independently.

Parallel Parking:

Parallel parking is a technique for parking a vehicle in line with other vehicles on the side of the road,facing in the same direction as traffic.It's often considered a difficult skill for new drivers to learn,but with practice it can become second nature.

Perpendicular Parking:

Perpendicular parking is a common parking method where a car is parked at a 90-degree algle to the curb or a building.Perpendicular parking is also known as nose-in parking,is the most common technique used in parking lots.

Angle Parking:

Angle parking is a parking method where vehicles are parked at an angle to the curb,usualy at 45-degree angle.It is also known asEchelon parking in Britan.Angle parking can be more efficient than parallel parking because it uses less road space by narrowingthe travel lanes. It can also make parking easier for drivers.
